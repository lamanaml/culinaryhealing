Live/Raw Foods

Uncooked Fruits and Veggies

What does it mean?

Live / raw foods are whole foods that still have the vitamins, minerals, phytochemicals, and enzymes intact. What are Enzymes?

  • Enzymes are the life force that conducts and directs every activity in your body.
  • Enzymes "digest" or break down food in our digestive systems.
  • Eating an enzyme-rich diet is thought to increase vitality, slow the aging process, help repair our DNA and RNA, break down proteins, increase weight loss and eliminate toxins.

Enzymes are heat sensitive and destroyed at temperatures above 118 degrees. This is important because the purpose of eating is to provide our bodies with these nutrients and enzymes, and if they are destroyed through cooking, we are not giving our bodies what it needs... which defeats the point... right?

So, during this phase, we will turn off the stoves and flood ourselves with nutrients and enzymes.

What can we eat?

In general, our diets should consist of at least 50% live foods. Eating live can mean something as simple as eating an apple or a salad, or can be so sophisticated that you will forget that it is raw! Most of the recipes that are listed in our forum are very simple, requiring basic equipment (knives, blenders or food processors) but if you have other equipment like dehydrators, mandolins or masticating juicers, I hope you will find the courage to find recipes that use them. For those of you who are not that familiar with live foods, you may be surprised with how much you enjoy them.

  • Fresh fruits (no seedless fruits):
    Citrus, Bananas, Berries, Coconuts, Tropical Fruit, Melons
  • Fresh veggies
    Green Leafy Vegetables, Root Vegetables, Peppers, Mushrooms, Sea Vegetables (Nori, Dulse, Sea Moss, and Arame)
  • Fermented Food
    Sauerkraut, Kimchee, Miso
  • Sprouts
    Alfalfa Sprouts, Bean Sprouts, Sprouted grains
  • Oils
    Grapeseed, Avocado, Extra Virgin Olive, Coconut
  • Dried fruits
    Sun-dried Tomatoes, Figs, Dates, Raisins, Cranberries, Currants - Try to find varieties with no sugar added
  • Raw Seeds
    Sesame, Pumpkin, Sunflower, Hemp
  • Salts And Salty Stuff
    Sea Salt, Crystal Salt, Liquid Aminos, Tamari, Shoyu
  • Sweeteners
    Honey, Maple Syrup, Agave Nectar, And Stevia
  • Herbs and Spices

What should we avoid?

Any fruit or veggie that is warmed/dehydrated at a temperature greater than 116 degrees.

Specifically, during the whole foods phase, should avoid

  • ALL COOKED FOOD
  • Nuts
  • Packaged, boxed or processed juices
  • Animal Protein
    Meat, Fish, Poultry, Eggs
  • Processed Vegetarian Protein
    Tofu, Tempeh, And Other Mock Meats
  • Nuts
  • Dairy
    Milk, Cheese, Butter, Yogurt, Kefir
  • Fast Food
  • All Processed Foods
    Bread, Pasta, White Rice, White Sugar, Table Salt, Canned Foods
  • Artificial Ingredients
  • Artificial Colors
  • Hydrogenated Oils
    Margarine, Crisco, partially hydrogenated oil
  • MSG also know as Monosodium Glutamate
  • Caffeine
    Coffee, teas with caffeine, Soda
  • Drugs and Alcohol
