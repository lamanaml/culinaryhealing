Whole Food

Uncooked Fruits and Vegetables

This initial phase of the cleanse is to help get everyone on the same page. We all have different dietary backgrounds (vegetarian, carnivore, vegan, SAD, pescatarian, raw... ), so this is our opportunity to sync our meals so that we can move forward as a group. Whole foods are foods that are as close to nature as they can be. Think produce aisle, bean aisle, whole grain aisle. These foods have not been processed or have been minimally altered. Whole foods can be cooked When we eat food that has been processed our bodies are not sure how to handle it. Much of the fiber and nutritional value have been removed. When our bodies do not know how to process something, it treats it like a toxin, which sometimes gets stored to be dealt with later. Let's say it together "NO MORE PROCESSED FOOD!" Note: Cooked food is allowed for the first two days. You can also go ahead and eat raw (uncooked) these first two days. Foods to Eliminate Starting Friday (Day 1), eliminate meat, fish, poultry, eggs, tofu, tempeh, and other mock meats nuts eggs and dairy fast food all processed foods - bread, pasta, white rice, white sugar, table salt, canned foods artificial ingredients, hydrogenated oils, MSG Coffee, caffeine alcohol and soda drugs What can we eat? So, now that we know what we can't eat, what's left? Note that we have upgraded this list to include more alkaline ingredients. These ingredients are better choices than most: ​ Fresh veggies: for example, green leafy vegetables, root vegetables, Squashes, Peppers, Okra, Onions and ginger, Sea Vegetables, Mushrooms... Sea Vegetables​ Nori, Kombu, Dulse, Sea Palm, Sea Moss, Hijiki, Wakame, Arame, Fresh fruits (no seedless fruits): Avocado, Tomatoes, Cucumbers, Limes, Bananas (try to find burro bananas), Green Bananas (use like potatoes), all Berries, Coconuts, Mangoes, Papaya, Cherries, Peaches, Melons, Figs... Whole grains: Brown rice, Farro, Buckwheat, Wild Rice, Quinoa, Millet, Spelt, Kamut, Teff, Amaranth, Rye Dried fruits and Sundried Tomatoes Seeds: sesame seeds, pumpkin seeds, sunflower seeds, hemp seeds Beans: chickpeas, lentils, black beans, peas, red beans, kidney beans... any bean Oils: Grapeseed Oil, Avocado Oil, Extra Virgin Olive Oil, Coconut Oil (unrefined has a coconut taste, refined has a neutral taste - both are OK) Salts and salty stuff Sea salt, Himalayan crystal salt, Celtic salt, liquid aminos, tamari, shoyu Sweeteners: Raw honey, Maple syrup, Dates, Dried fruit, Agave nectar, and Stevia Herbal Teas (dandelion and milk thistle are my favorites) Any fresh herbs or dried spices A note about carbohydrates There are two types of carbohydrates, refined and complex. Refined carbs started off as a whole food, but have been stripped of much of the fiber and nutritional content. This includes white rice, white pasta, white sugar, white bread, most commercially available cereals etc. Because the fiber has been removed it enters your bloodstream quickly, spikes your blood sugar and leaves you feeling depleted soon after. On the other hand complex carbs (which we can and should eat) are complete unprocessed whole foods and enter your bloodstream more gradually giving you sustained energy. These are whole grains like oats, brown rice, quinoa, millet, farro and starchy vegetables like potatoes. Do not let the advertising on foods trick you. The only foods that are whole grains are actually whole grains. Bread, whole wheat bagels, cereals, etc. are NOT whole grains.
